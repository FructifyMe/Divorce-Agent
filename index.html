Process

1A vs. 1B Divorce

1A (joint/uncontested): both agree it's over + a signed notarized Separation Agreement. ~4–6 months.

1B (contested): one wants it, or terms unresolved. Hearing no sooner than 6 months after filing. ~12–18 months.

No-fault = "irretrievable breakdown." Fault grounds exist but are rare.

Jurisdiction

Residency & Venue

File in MA if you've lived here 1 year, or the breakdown happened here while living here as a couple.

Essex venue: Lawrence court if either party lives in Methuen, Lawrence, Andover, North Andover, or Haverhill; otherwise Salem.

Rule 411

Automatic Restraining Order

Kicks in on filing (plaintiff) / service (defendant). While pending, neither party may:

  • Sell, hide, or transfer marital assets (beyond ordinary expenses)
  • Run up unreasonable debt on the other's credit
  • Drop spouse/kids from insurance or change beneficiaries
Disclosure

Financial Statement & Disclosure

Rule 401: sworn Financial Statement within 45 days of service. Income <$75K → Short Form (CJD-301S); ≥$75K → Long Form (CJD-301L).

Rule 410: auto-exchange 3 yrs taxes, pay stubs, account statements within 45 days.

Coach tip Helping clients organize this is your highest-value work.

Property §34

Equitable Distribution

MA divides property equitably (fairly), not necessarily 50/50. Judge weighs many §34 factors (length of marriage, contributions, conduct, needs…).

Broad estate: nearly everything can be on the table regardless of title — including inheritances, gifts, and premarital assets (depending on marriage length & commingling).

A professional degree/license is not divisible property.

Marital Home

Options for the House

  • Sell & split net equity
  • Buyout — one keeps it, compensates the other (refinance or asset offset)
  • Defer sale / nesting — e.g. until kids finish school
Retirement

Retirement & QDROs

A QDRO is a separate court order to split a 401(k)/pension — plans won't divide without one. DRO for MA state/municipal plans; COAP for federal.

Done right → tax-free, penalty-free transfer between spouses.

Debt

Dividing Debt

Debts divided equitably by who incurred them & why. Caution: a "hold-harmless" clause doesn't bind creditors — they can still pursue the other spouse. Best fix: refinance/pay off/remove names.

Alimony

Alimony — Types & Limits

Four types: general term, rehabilitative, reimbursement (≤5 yr marriages), transitional (≤5 yr).

Duration (general term): ≤5 yr→50% of months; 5–10→60%; 10–15→70%; 15–20→80%; 20+→indefinite.

Amount: generally ≤ recipient's need or 30–35% of the income difference.

Child Support

Child Support (2025 Guidelines)

New guidelines effective Dec 1, 2025. Worksheet = CJD-304. Income cap $450K; child-care benchmark $430/wk; min order $15/wk.

Based on both incomes, parenting time, health insurance, child care. Runs to 18 (up to 21/23 if dependent/in college).

Parent Ed

"Two Families Now"

Required co-parenting course for minor-children cases without agreement. 4-hr online, $49/parent. Register within 30 days of service. Waiver = CJD-444.

Essex Court

Essex Probate & Family Court

Salem (main): 36 Federal St, Salem · (978) 744-1020

Lawrence: 2 Appleton St · (978) 686-9692 (Methuen/Lawrence/Andover/N.Andover/Haverhill)

Note No Newburyport divorce court — those go to Salem.

eFile: efilema.com (attorneys required; pro se optional).

Fees

Filing Fees

1A total $215 · 1B total $220. Can't afford it → Affidavit of Indigency (waives court + $49 parent-ed fees).

Forms

Key Forms

  • CJD-101A Joint Petition (1A)
  • CJD-101B Complaint (1B)
  • CJD-301S/301L Financial Statement
  • CJD-304 Child Support Worksheet
  • R-408 Record of Absolute Divorce (not the CS worksheet!)
Taxes

Tax Flags

Spousal transfers generally tax-free (IRC §1041) but carry the original basis — compare after-tax value. Home-sale exclusion $250K single/$500K joint (§121). Alimony post-2018: not taxed/not deductible.

Modification

Changing Orders Later

Child support: modify on material change OR if the order is just inconsistent with current guidelines (lower bar).

Alimony: material change only; ends on remarriage, 3+ mo cohabitation, or payer's full retirement age.

Three Things That Surprise People

"Equitable" ≠ equal

MA splits property fairly, which isn't always 50/50 — and almost everything can be considered, even inheritances or premarital assets.

There's a waiting period

Even after a judge signs off, the divorce isn't final for ~90–120 days. You can't remarry until it's absolute.

Joint debt can follow you

If your agreement says your ex pays a joint debt but they don't, the lender can still come after you. Remove your name where possible.

Common client questions. General information, not legal advice — specific situations go to the client's attorney.
How long does a divorce take?

Uncontested (1A) often 4–6 months; contested (1B) 12–18 months. Even after approval there's a ~90–120 day waiting period before it's final.

How much does it cost to file?

About $215 (1A) or $220 (1B). If money is tight, an Affidavit of Indigency can waive court fees. Attorney costs are separate.

Do I need a lawyer?

Not required, but divorce involves binding decisions — at minimum have an attorney review your agreement. A coach helps you prepare so attorney time is used well.

Where will my case be heard?

Essex County → Salem, or Lawrence if either spouse lives in Methuen, Lawrence, Andover, North Andover, or Haverhill. No Newburyport divorce court.

Can I file if my spouse won't agree?

Yes — MA has no-fault divorce. If they don't agree it's a contested (1B) case with a built-in waiting period.

Will I get half of everything?

Not necessarily. MA divides property equitably (fairly), which isn't always equally.

Is my inheritance protected?

Maybe — in MA almost everything can be considered, though inheritances/premarital assets are often kept with the original owner depending on marriage length and whether they were mixed into shared finances.

What happens to the house?

Common options: sell and split, one spouse buys out the other, or delay the sale (sometimes until kids finish school).

What is the "automatic restraining order"?

Not about safety — it's a financial freeze on filing that stops either spouse from selling off/hiding assets or dropping insurance while the case is pending.

How is child support calculated?

An official state worksheet based on both incomes, parenting time, health insurance, and child-care costs. Guidelines updated Dec 1, 2025 — use the current calculator.

Can support or custody change later?

Yes, on a significant change in circumstances. Child support can also be revisited if it no longer matches current guidelines.

Can I remarry right away?

No — only after the divorce becomes absolute (final), following the post-judgment waiting period.
